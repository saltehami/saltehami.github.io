This page is a working shelf, not a canon. It should change as the questions change.

## Strategy and firms

- Stratechery, especially the habit of separating business structure from surface narrative.
- Case studies that make the constraints visible before they make the recommendation.
- Company histories where the operating model matters more than the slogan.

## Healthcare organizations

- Writing that treats clinics, payors, vendors, and regulators as operating systems with incentives.
- Anything that explains how work actually moves: forms, queues, audits, staffing, billing, documentation, exceptions.

## AI systems and infrastructure

- Evals, retrieval, observability, workflow design, and deployment notes.
- Postmortems where the failure mode is specific enough to learn from.
- Builder writing that explains what changed after the demo.

## Personal references

- Patrick Collison's plain, link-forward personal site.
- Research lab notebooks, technical blogs, and public work logs that keep their rough edges.
